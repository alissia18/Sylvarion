Identity And Ideology

If this were to happen, I would be disappointed, but not surprised. Sadly, the gender wage gap is a commonality in the workplace. Still, holding concrete evidence in my hands would give me the courage to try and make a change in the company.

First, I would go to other coworkers and show them the paper. Generally these claims will be taken more seriously when made by a group than as an individual. Ideally, I would get some men on my side too as men are generally taken more seriously than women. In the perfect world where men would agree to help me take action against this injustice, we would then work together to collect more evidence before bringing the case to our higher-ups. Of course, I would be asking the higher-ups to equalize the wage gap between men and women doing the same job. Sadly, there is a high chance my boss or even my male coworkers would dismiss me as being too dramatic or pushy, as discussed in “The Dads of Tech”. Clay Shirky thinks women simply aren’t willing to self-promote but the reality is that when we try to, we get dismissed for being too emotional. I believe this is because the men who own the tech industry have not changed their beliefs about women in programming. As stated in On Software, or the Persistence of Visual Knowledge, the ideal vision of programming in the early days was a man calling out commands to a female operator.

The men in charge of the tech industry tend to dismiss women’s role in technological development but, as stated in On Software, or the Persistence of Visual Knowledge, women were “the simulators, assemblers and programmers” of early computers. Technology would not be where it is today without its female operators, but for some reason, men are inclined to ignore this history and treat female programmers as nothing more than mindless machines, as was done to the Wrens – female programmer volunteers from the Women’s Royal Naval Service – in the past. I wish I could say I believed in my chances of earning equal pay for women and men, but the reality is that my claim would likely be ignored by my boss and nothing would change.
